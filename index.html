Block-level elements ... most significant characteristic is that they typically are formatted with a line break before and after the element (thereby creating a stand-alone block of content). That is, they take up the width of their containers. (MDN HTML Reference)

Block level elements take up 100% of the width of the parent container. (Unless a width is specified.) They can be assigned width, height, margin and padding.

Block level elements are automatically placed on the next line after the previous block level element. This is the normal document flow when you don't use positioning or floats.
